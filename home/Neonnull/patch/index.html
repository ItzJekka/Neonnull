Patch Notes Pre-Alpha V0.36

Release Date: April 21, 2026

Welcome to the latest patch notes for Neon Null! In this update, we've made significant improvements to the game's performance and stability, as well as added new features and content. Here's a breakdown of what's new:

  • New:
    • Added a new offensive module, the Neon Railgun.
    • Added a new cosmic blessing, Rail Loop.
    • Added a new cosmic+ upgrade, Secure Loop.
    • Added a new tech upgrade, Charging Imperative.
    • Added a new tech upgrade, Overdrive Capacitors.
    • Added a new 'System Health' mechanic. Now, instead of bosses spawning at specific levels, they will spawn once the system health drops to 0. This is a standard timer with a bit of a twist, and it allows for good performance to be rewarded by being able to go in to a boss at a higher level than normal. This will get expanded on as time goes on.
  • Changes:
    • Added a little bit of animation to the HP and EXP bars in the cluster upgrade.
    • Adjusted avoidance logic amongst a handful of enemy types.
    • Removed lancing module blessing and made it a default part of Neon Lance by default.
    • Slightly adjusted base regeneration rate and regeneration delay time for boosting, making it regenerate approximately 10-15% faster.
    • Many small volume tweaks in areas where they were a little too loud.
    • Major skill tree visual pass (removed panel from right side, added hoverable tooltip, many other general improvements).
    • Moderate settings menu visual pass. Many changes, tweaks, etc.
    • Added new sprites for the three different CPUs (placeholders, but more pleasant to look at).
    • Added new sprites for the three different drives (placeholders, but more pleasant to look at).
    • Moderate 'game over' screen overhaul.
    • Adjusted boss spawn logic so their intro animation plays immediately instead of there being a weird delay.
  • Fixes:
    • Fixed an inconsistency with the thrusters upgrade causing common to give more than uncommon.
    • Fixed a bug that caused players to get two upgrades when materializing on controller.
    • Fixed a bug with rarity colors in the upgrade menu not matching the card itself on controller.
    • Fixed a bug where boss damage was projectile damage instead of collision damage (and provided no knockback).

Thank you for playing Neon Null! We hope you enjoy the new content and improvements. As always, we welcome your feedback and suggestions for future updates.
